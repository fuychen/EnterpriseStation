About us
Provision
The story can be traced back to 10 years ago when Niki Wang (co-founder of PZ Interculture) took part in an English summer camp to Australia. That two-week summer camp left a great impact on her life and made her realize that study tour is a perfect way to meet different people and cultures.
Bearing that in mind, Niki went to the United States to pursue her study. For over 6 years in school, Niki has devoted herself in promoting cultural communication between students of China and America whether as a language partner or the chairman of student union.
After graduating from the University of Maryland, she came back to homeland and set out to make preparations for her first culture project wholeheartedly. Gradually, like-minded fellows joined in. Most of them have experiences of studying abroad. Throughout continuous efforts, 2017 PZ “A Bite of Beijing” study tour was born. This study tour, which you may attend, not only condensed Niki’s greatest enthusiasm for both Oriental and Western Culture, but also embodied our team’s biggest effort as well as wisdom.
It is our expectation that you will know the real China after participating in our program. When the charm of Orient sparks in your eyes, the dream of our eternal pursuit comes true.
Staff
Niki Wang
co-founder of PZ interculture
Niki went to the Garrison Forest School as a high school student, and received her Bachelor’ s degree from the University of Maryland with the major of Economy. For over 6 years in America, she has got plenty chances to communicate with people from different backgrounds, and this has helped build her tolerant personality. She has always been curious about the world and found it rather interesting when diverse cultures clashed. She was the yearbook editor when she was a senior student in GFS. In junior year of college, she became the president of Chinese Student and Scholar Association. She seized opportunities to get in touch with different cultures and made her life colorful. Her passion for intercultural communication grows stronger when she returned China. After careful consideration, she decided to found her own company to carry out cultural exchange programs. With more excellent members joining in, the first program emerged. Niki always finds it enchanting to integrate different cultures and she believes that “A Bite of Beijing” study tour is a perfect way.
